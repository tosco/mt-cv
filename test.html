the Loomstate difference

The average cotton tshirt is a product of a global journey thousands of miles long from cotton harvest, through multiple countries for thread-knitting and cloth-cutting before reaching a store shelf. This journey, and the average tee-making process, is not always transparent, nor is it always sustainable.

That is where Loomstate comes in. Founded by Scott Mackinlay-Hahn and Rogan Gregory in 2004 and headquartered in New York City, Loomstate is committed making quality clothing to create a sustainable future. Now, we are excited to present a new tee transparently produced, grown, and sewn entirely in America. Click here to be one of the first to known when our tees are grown and sewn and ready for you. Read on to learn more...

making the most traceable tee in the world

Loomstate tees are super soft, built to last, made from high quality organic cotton, but so are a lot of other tees. What makes our tees stand above other tees on the market, organic included, is beyond what you can see and feel. It's about what you know about the shirt, your awareness of it's environmental impact, the connection you have to the community that made this one garment happen. We seek to provide our customers with full transparency of our supply chain and to establish sustainable relationships with each step of the production process.

At Loomstate, we asked ourselves, "Can we make a truly sustainable tshirt out of organic cotton? Can we make a best-practice, ethical, environmentally sound garment?" Then, we pushed ourselves a little further..."Now, can we make this all happen in America?"

Turns out, that we can...and so we did. Loomstate partnered with TS Designs to produce the most traceable tshirt, grown and sewn in America. The journey took us from a cotton farm, to the cutting floor, and finally to a glass of milk. Follow our journey.

organic cotton farm

Our cotton is grown by Phillip and Orpha Gene Watson of Hickory Meadows Organics located in Whitakers, North Carolina. See the farmers in action.

organic cotton gin

The cotton is sent just down the road to Whitakers Gin, where workers use machines called cotton gins to separate cottonseed from the cotton fiber. Take a look at the process here. From the gin, they go separate ways...by the end of this journey, you will see where they end up.

cotton spinning

Next, the cotton fiber is spun in to thread at Hill Spinning in Thomasville, NC. The raw fiber gets carded, combed, spun and spooled.

cotton knitting

The finished thread is knit in to fabric by the folks at Mortex Apparel in Wendell, North Carolina.

cut, sew, & trim

The organic cotton fabric leaves North Carolina and is cut and sewn into a tshirt at Fig Apparel Solutions in Cerritos, California. See where the action happens.

cotton dyeing

The cotton is dyed just down the way at Harry's Dye & Wash Co in Anaheim, California. This grown and sewn in America shirt is nearly complete!

printing

One final stop before the tshirts reach the shop. Econo Graphics in Brooklyn, NY print our graphics using water-based and non-toxic inks. Some shirts may be printed with other local printers.

dairy

Remember the cottonseed that was removed from the cotton fibers back at Whitakers Gin in North Carolina? Well, they were sent down the road to George Teague of Reedy Fork Farms in Elon, North Carolina. Here, his dairy cows eat the cottonseed. Take a look!

milk processing

Milk from these cows is cooled and transported a bit further west to Milko Inc in Asheville, North Carolina where it becomes an Organic Valley milk product. From here, the milk products are distributed to store shelves throughout the North East.
